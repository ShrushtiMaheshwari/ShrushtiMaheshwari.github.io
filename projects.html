You can find some of the projects I have worked on below.

  • Nonlinear Viscoelastic Creep Modeling of Heat-Treated FDM PLA
    • Developed and validated a nonlinear Burgers-based constitutive model incorporating stress-, temperature-, and crystallinity-dependent parameters to capture creep loading–unloading behavior of semi-crystalline polymers.
    • Performed multi-curve parameter calibration in MATLAB (SQP-based optimization) across 27–47 °C and 8–12 MPa; achieved R² ≈ 0.98 against experimental data.
    • Integrated Schapery nonlinear viscoelastic framework into Burgers compliance formulation to model stress-dependent creep at elevated temperatures.
    • Linked microstructural evolution (3% → 53% crystallinity via DSC) to constitutive parameters, enabling physics-informed prediction of long-term deformation behavior.
  • Thermo-Mechanical Modeling of Heat-Treated 3D-Printed PLA Scaffolds
    • Developed a processing–structure–property framework linking DSC-derived crystallinity (3% → ~54%) to compressive yield enhancement (~20% increase) in FDM-printed PLA scaffolds (HT90(120)-70 condition).
    • Performed temperature-dependent and physiologically relevant compression testing (27°C vs 37°C; dry vs wet), generating validated stress–strain datasets for nonlinear material calibration.
    • Characterized surface morphology evolution (Sa: ~25 µm → ~44 µm) and correlated roughness-driven microstructural changes with biodegradation and mechanical response.
    • Established constitutive insight into cyclic unloading–reloading behavior, demonstrating improved elastic recovery and deformation resistance in recrystallized PLA scaffolds.
  • Nonlinear Cyclic Constitutive Modeling of FDM-Printed PLA
    • Developed a thermo-mechanically coupled viscoplastic constitutive model capturing cyclic loading–unloading behavior of semi-crystalline PLA, incorporating internal variable evolution and strain-rate dependence.
    • Implemented and validated a multi-network framework (amorphous + crystalline phases) to capture hysteresis, stiffness evolution, and cyclic softening/hardening under compression.
    • Calibrated model parameters via nonlinear optimization in MATLAB and achieved high-fidelity agreement with experimental stress–strain loops across multiple strain rates and temperatures.
    • Structured formulation for 3D finite element implementation (UMAT/VUMAT-ready), enabling predictive simulation of time-dependent deformation in load-bearing polymer components.
  • Frequency–Temperature Master Curve Modeling of Glass–Epoxy Composites
    • Conducted DMA frequency sweeps (0.1–10 Hz, 30–110 °C) on neat epoxy and optimized glass–epoxy composites (15% spherical, 10% milled fiber); identified Tg ≈ 73 °C and generated master curves spanning 10⁻⁶–10⁸ Hz via WLF-based TTS.
    • Quantified filler-geometry effects: 10% milled fiber composite achieved ~65% higher storage modulus (E′) than neat epoxy at high reduced frequencies due to enhanced polymer chain entanglement.
    • Developed a 14-term Prony series viscoelastic model with skewed Gaussian relaxation spectrum, achieving R² ≈ 0.98–0.99 across materials for master curve fitting.
    • Correlated SEM-observed filler–matrix debonding and microstructural deformation with reduced E′ at elevated temperatures, linking micro-mechanics to constitutive parameters.
  • EEG-Controlled Pneumatic Medical Bed (Brain–Computer Interface Prototype)
    • Designed and built a non-contact BCI-based control system enabling patients to raise/lower a pneumatically actuated hospital bed using EEG signals and machine learning classification (lift up, lower down, comfort pose).
    • Integrated EEG headset, ML signal processing, Node-RED logic, 8-bit DAQ, power amplifier, proportional DC valve, and double-acting pneumatic cylinder into a closed-loop electro-pneumatic architecture.
    • Implemented a PID-controlled position feedback system (magnetic cylinder sensor) to achieve stable, low-error actuator positioning under compressible air dynamics.
    • Experimentally validated on multiple users after 16-hour training cycles, demonstrating reliable thought-to-actuation translation and repeatable comfort-position control.
  • Laser-Based Post-Processing of Additive Manufactured Components
    • Authored a comprehensive technical review on laser polishing and laser shock peening for AM metals and polymers, covering SLM, EBM, FDM, WAAM, and Binder Jet processes.
    • Analyzed defect mechanisms (balling, stair-stepping, residual tensile stress, porosity) and mapped them to optimized laser-based mitigation strategies.
    • Compared continuous-wave, pulsed, and femtosecond laser polishing mechanisms, highlighting melt-pool dynamics, capillary flow, and selective ablation regimes.
    • Synthesized roughness and fatigue data across Ti-6Al-4V, CoCr, SS316L, AlSi10Mg, and Inconel alloys, demonstrating up to ~90%+ surface roughness reduction and significant compressive residual stress induction via LSP.
  • Deep Neural Network for Predicting Strength of Dissimilar Friction Stir Welds
    • Developed a 5-layer feed-forward, backpropagated DNN to predict ultimate tensile strength (UTS) and weld efficiency of dissimilar Al–Ti friction stir welds using rotation speed, weld speed, thickness, and base material strength as inputs.
    • Performed hyperparameter optimization (Adam, LogCosh loss, mixed activation functions) and data normalization to capture strong nonlinearity in weld physics and microstructural evolution.
    • Achieved R² = 0.95 (training) and 0.90 (testing) on literature dataset; experimental validation on Al–Ti welds demonstrated R² ≈ 0.91–0.98, confirming predictive robustness.
    • Experimentally validated model predictions via ASTM E8 tensile testing and SEM microstructural characterization, correlating Ti particle fragmentation, heat input, and weld quality with predicted mechanical performance.
  • Bio-Printing Inks & Process Compatibility for 3D Tissue Engineering
    • Conducted a comprehensive technical review mapping bio-ink material properties (viscosity, gelation rate, surface tension, UV stability) to major bioprinting platforms including FDM, SLA/DLP, DIW, Laser-Guided Writing, and Inkjet printing.
    • Analyzed process–material compatibility constraints, defining quantitative ink requirements (e.g., FDM melt viscosity & temperature ranges, SLA optical transparency, Inkjet viscosity <10 cP, DIW gelation kinetics).
    • Evaluated leading bio-inks such as PCL, PLA, PEEK, PPF, PDLLA, GelMA-PEGDA hydrogels, correlating mechanical strength, degradation behavior, and cell viability with specific printing mechanisms.
    • Identified critical engineering trade-offs between resolution, mechanical integrity, degradation kinetics, and cell survival, outlining pathways for multi-material and gene-activated bio-ink development.
  • Heat-Treatment Optimization of 3D-Printed PLA Scaffolds for Enhanced Biomechanics & Biocompatibility
    • Designed and fabricated ASTM D695-compliant solid PLA scaffolds using FDM, followed by systematic post-processing heat treatment (60–105°C; 0.5–2 h) to optimize structure–property relationships.
    • Increased crystallinity from ~3% (untreated) to ~53% via controlled annealing at 90°C for 2 h, validated through DSC thermal analysis.
    • Improved compressive yield strength from ~65 MPa to ~85 MPa and transformed post-yield behavior from strain softening to strain hardening.
    • Demonstrated enhanced biological performance: cell viability improved from ~64% to ~96%, with uniform cell spreading enabled by heat-induced micron-scale surface morphologies.
  • Indigenous 3-DoF & 6-DoF Motion Platform for Gaming & Training Simulators

  • Thermo–Mechanical Viscoplastic Modeling of FDM-Printed PLA Under Compression
    • Designed and executed temperature- (25–50°C) and strain rate-dependent (2.1×10⁻³–2.1×10⁻² s⁻¹) compression experiments on ASTM D695 specimens; identified Tg ≈ 55°C via DMA and established clear thermo–rate coupling in yield and post-yield behavior.
    • Developed a finite-strain thermo-mechanical viscoplastic constitutive model (Arruda–Boyce hyperelastic network + viscous dashpot) capable of capturing strain softening and rate-dependent flow using a single parameter set.
    • Implemented the model in MATLAB using constrained SQP optimization (fmincon) for simultaneous multi-condition calibration; achieved overall R² ≈ 0.98 between experimental and predicted stress–strain responses.
    • Correlated SEM-observed micro-damage mechanisms (void growth, filament fragmentation, twist) with global stress–strain trends, enabling physics-informed prediction of PLA performance in temperature-sensitive applications.
  • IoT-Based Smart School Bus Management System (End-to-End Architecture Design)

    • Developed complete sensor placement strategy and circuit-level integration, including GPS tracking (Neo-6M), RFID attendance (RC522), gas detection (MQ2), oxygen monitoring, vibration sensing, and real-time camera streaming.
    • Implemented a publish–subscribe MQTT communication framework with SQL cloud database for real-time stakeholder access (school, parents, transporters, drivers) and threshold-based alert management.
    • Proposed data-driven optimization using machine learning (e.g., logistic regression for punctuality profiling) to improve route efficiency, safety, maintenance planning, and stakeholder feedback integration.
  • Laser Shock Peening (LSP) of FDM-Printed PLA for Mechanical Reinforcement
    • Designed and executed a parameter-optimized laser shock peening (LSP) study on FDM-printed PLA using a Q-switched Nd:YAG laser (up to 257 mJ), systematically varying pulse energy, translation speed, and repetition rate to control fluence and shock pressure.
    • Achieved ~45% improvement in compressive plateau stress (from ~55 MPa untreated to ~78 MPa) under optimized conditions (257 mJ, 5 mm/s, 10 Hz) without thermal degradation.
    • Modeled and quantified fluence, peak shock pressure (up to ~3.97 GPa), pulse overlap, and interaction time, linking laser parameters to subsurface plastic deformation and residual stress development.
    • Validated microstructural changes via optical microscopy, demonstrating improved interlayer bonding and void closure under optimal settings, while identifying burn-induced degradation under excessive heat accumulation.
  • Strain Rate & Infill Density Effects on Compressive Behavior of FDM-Printed PLA
    • Conducted systematic compression testing on FDM-printed PLA across multiple strain rates and infill densities, establishing clear coupling between internal architecture and rate-dependent mechanical response.
    • Demonstrated that increasing infill density significantly enhances yield strength, stiffness, and energy absorption, while lower densities amplify strain-rate sensitivity due to internal void-driven deformation mechanisms.
    • Quantified progressive deformation modes (cell wall buckling, filament fracture, interlayer debonding) using stress–strain analysis and microstructural observations to explain architecture-driven mechanical trends.
    • Established design guidelines linking print architecture (infill %) → deformation mechanism → compressive strength, enabling performance tuning of lightweight FDM components for structural applications.
